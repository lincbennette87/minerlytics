Stage-Specific KPI Dashboard

Different goals require different metrics.

Focus Explorer Developer Producer
Primary Objective Discover and define an economic deposit. Advance a deposit into a financeable mine. Generate profitable production and cash flow.
Key KPIs
Healthy Signs ✓ Growing resources, strong assays, and 12 to 24 months of funding. ✓ Strong economics, advancing permits, and clear financing path. ✓ Positive FCF, low AISC, reserve replacement, and guidance discipline.
Warning Signs ▲ Dilution, weak drill results, stagnant resources, or low cash. ▲ CapEx inflation, delays, weak economics, or financing uncertainty. ▲ Rising AISC, shrinking reserves, missed guidance, or negative FCF.
Valuation Focus Discovery potential and ounces found. Project economics and de-risking progress. Cash flow, earnings, FCF, and shareholder returns.
Revenue / Cash Flow Revenue not expected; negative cash flow. Usually little to no revenue; negative cash flow. Revenue required; positive cash flow expected.

Explorer = Ounces Found

Value comes from discovery potential, drill success, resource growth, and funding runway.

Developer = Project Economics

Value comes from de-risking the project through studies, permits, financing, and construction readiness.

Producer = Cash Flow Generated

Value comes from production, cost control, free cash flow, reserves, and shareholder returns.

Risk generally decreases as a project advances, while valuation shifts from discovery potential to sustainable cash flow.

How an Explorer Becomes a Developer

An exploration-stage miner is trying to prove a discovery exists and is valuable. Each milestone reduces uncertainty and helps investors understand whether the project could become a future mine.

01

Explorer

Find a deposit and prove it has potential.

→
02

Developer

Study, permit, finance, and prepare to build.

→
03

Producer

Mine and sell minerals.

Major Explorer Milestones

Select a milestone to see the question being answered, the work required, and why it matters.

Milestone 1

Acquire Land

Question: Do we control the ground?

The company secures the mineral rights or claims where it believes a valuable deposit may exist.

Work Required

Stake claims, acquire mineral rights, review historical data, secure basic exploration access, and confirm ownership boundaries.

Why It Matters

Without control of the ground, there is no real project. This is the foundation for every future exploration dollar spent.

Simple translation: Explorer means the company is trying to prove a mineral discovery exists, is large enough, and may be valuable enough to become a mine.

How a Mining Developer Progresses

A developer-stage miner sits between discovery and production. Its job is to prove the project can be built, permitted, financed, and operated.

01

Explorer

Find metal and define the deposit.

→
02

Developer

Turn a discovery into a build-ready mine.

→
03

Producer

Mine and sell minerals.

Major Developer Milestones

Select a milestone to see what work is done and why it matters.

Milestone 1

Resource Defined

The company estimates how much metal is in the ground and whether the deposit is large enough to justify more advanced studies.

Work Required

Drill the deposit, assay the samples, model the geology, and publish a resource estimate.

Why It Matters

A defined resource gives investors the first real evidence that the project could become an economic mine.

Simple translation: Developer means the company has a deposit and is working through studies, permits, financing, and construction before becoming a producer.
