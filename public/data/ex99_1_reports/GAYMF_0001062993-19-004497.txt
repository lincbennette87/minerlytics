 Item 4:

 On November 7, 2019, Asanko announced that it was
 recognizing an impairment of US$128.3 million against its equity
 investment in the Asanko Gold Mine ("AGM") joint venture (the
 "AGM JV") as a result of ongoing work associated with its
 updated AGM life of mine plan (the "AGM LOM"). 
 
 
 
 
 Item 5: 
 
 
 
 
 
 5.1 
 Full Description of Material
 Change 
 
 
 
 
 
 On November 7, 2019, Asanko announced that it
 was recognizing an impairment of US$128.3 million against its equity
 investment in the AGM JV as a result of ongoing work associated with the
 AGM LOM. The AGM JV is jointly controlled with Gold Fields Limited. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 The impairment was based on management's estimate of the
 recoverable amount of the AGM, which took into account the latest
 available information from the ongoing work associated with the AGM LOM,
 which indicates that the target mine life and production is expected to
 result in the extraction of materially less than the total previously
 estimated reserves, and that the overall resource base for the AGM may be
 reduced considerably. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 The AGM JV has not yet finalized the AGM LOM and, as a
 result, the life of mine cash flow projections used in the impairment
 assessment are preliminary in nature. As the AGM LOM progresses, life of
 mine cash flow projections may change as a result of, among other things,
 changes to the current estimates of in-situ ounces, ore tonnes to be
 mined, strip ratios, head grades, recovery rates, gold prices, mining
 costs, processing costs, trucking costs, capital and closure costs and
 discount rates.
