Economic context‚ÄãIn 2024, the federal government promoted several bills and succeeded in approving some key regulations for Argentina's economic activity.‚ÄãThe federal government took actions to achieve fiscal balance, with a zero monetary emission policy for the whole public sector and a reduction in different expenses and subsidies. This has enabled the government to achieve, in a short management period, a primary and financial surplus in relation to gross domestic product (GDP), as well as to begin an inflation deceleration process. According to the indicators published by the INDEC (Argentine Statistics and Census Institute), retail inflation was 2.7% in December 2024, the total accumulated inflation for the year reached 117.8% accumulated in the 12 months of 2024. ‚ÄãThe following are areas of interest in the Argentine economic context:‚ÄãForeign exchange market‚ÄãArgentina currently has an extensive regulatory framework in foreign exchange matters, with different effects on access to foreign currency by the economic players. The foreign exchange system establishes a single and free foreign exchange market. According to their needs, these players also use other mechanisms to access foreign currency, giving rise to alternative exchange rates and creating a gap with the rate used in the single and free foreign exchange market (official exchange rate). In 2024, the so-called gap between the alternative exchange rates and the official rate has been significantly reduced. In December 2024, the gap remained below 10%. The current administration has relaxed foreign exchange regulations, but a significant part of the regulatory framework remains in place, generating the effects previously mentioned. ‚ÄãInvestment promotion plan‚ÄãThe foreign currency, financial and regulatory restrictions that have affected Argentina‚Äôs economy in recent years have discouraged investment; and one of the requirements for investment is that investors have expectations of being able to remit their profits freely. To mitigate this situation and promote the country‚Äôs productive matrix, while generating employment and fiscal resources, the government implemented the incentive system for large investments (RIGI, in Spanish), which will offer certain strategic sectors, capable of generating exports in the medium and long term, tax benefits, access to foreign currency for imports and, under certain conditions, the ability to remit profits. Additionally, a RIGI program for SMEs would be under way.‚ÄãThe government announced that the first formal RIGI projects have been submitted, totaling around USD 1,5 billion.‚ÄãThe government also reported that the World Bank and the Inter-American Development Bank (IADB) have committed to providing financing totaling USD 8,8 billion, out of which USD 3 billion would be received in the next two years to promote investments in strategic sectors such as renewable energy and sustainable mining, and USD 1,4 billion to support the Argentine private sector, while the remainder will strengthen social protection, improve primary education, optimize fiscal management, and expand access to essential services, with a special focus on assisting the most vulnerable sectors.‚ÄãForeign trade‚ÄãExports‚ÄãIn relation to the export incentive program, please refer to note 28.‚ÄãImports‚ÄãPresidential Decree No. 377/2023 extended to imports the payment of the PAIS tax (tax for an inclusive and supportive Argentina) imposed by Law No. 27,541.
